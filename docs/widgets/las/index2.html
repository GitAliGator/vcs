Local vs. Global Semantic Assessment

Global Alignment Score (GAS)

Captures broad thematic similarity using full-document embeddings. Excellent for overall narrative coherence.

Limitation: May not detect fine-grained semantic discrepancies, omitted details, or extraneous content at the local level.

Local Alignment Score (LAS)

Measures average semantic quality of matched chunk pairs identified by the Best Matching Algorithm. Quantifies fine-grained semantic coherence.

Strength: Detects local inconsistencies, missing details, and semantic misalignments that GAS might miss.

LAS Computation

Precision LAS
LAS_P = (1/|M_P|) × Σ Sim(c_gen, c_ref)
Average similarity of Gen→Ref matches
Recall LAS
LAS_R = (1/|M_R|) × Σ Sim(c_ref, c_gen)
Average similarity of Ref→Gen matches
Overall LAS
LAS = 2 × (LAS_P × LAS_R) / (LAS_P + LAS_R)
F1-style harmonic mean

      What's Next: Narrative Alignment Score (NAS)

      While LAS accurately measures local semantic similarities, it's inherently insensitive to chronological order and structural narrative integrity:

      • • Chronological Order: Events may be described accurately but presented out of sequence
      • • Narrative Structure: Significant narrative portions may be omitted despite high local similarities
      • • Spurious Content: LAS might remain high due to weak matches to irrelevant content
