Global Semantic Alignment

Global Alignment Score (GAS)

Measuring overall semantic alignment between entire reference and generated texts through global embedding comparison.

What is Global Alignment Score (GAS)?

The Global Alignment Score (GAS) measures the overall semantic alignment between entire reference texts (Tref) and generated texts (Tgen). GAS determines whether both narratives address similar or identical overarching themes, concepts, and entities at a holistic level.

How GAS Works

Step 1: Global Embedding

Both texts are embedded independently using nv-embed-v2 to generate global embedding vectors.

Step 2: Cosine Similarity

GAS is calculated using cosine similarity between these global embeddings.

Step 3: Holistic Assessment

Scores range from 0 to 1, with higher values indicating greater semantic alignment.

Mathematical Formulation

Step 1: Embedding Generation
Eref = EmbedNV2(Tref)
Egen = EmbedNV2(Tgen)
Step 2: Cosine Similarity
GAS = cos(Eref, Egen) =
(Eref · Egen) / (‖Eref‖ · ‖Egen‖)

GAS Strengths and Critical Limitations

What GAS Does Well

Semantic Alignment Recognition

Excellent at identifying when paraphrasing preserves meaning (0.897 score)

Length-Agnostic Evaluation

Handles both brevity and verbosity effectively (0.941 and 0.886 scores)

Thematic Coherence Assessment

Captures overall narrative themes regardless of expression style

Global Perspective

Provides holistic view of semantic alignment across entire texts

Critical Limitations

Chronological Blindness

Cannot detect local (0.991) or global (0.981) chronological disruptions

No Local Content Assessment

Global embeddings miss detailed event-level accuracy and coverage

Lacks Granular Analysis

Cannot identify specific content omissions or hallucinations

No Narrative Flow Evaluation

Insensitive to logical sequence and temporal progression

Why GAS Alone Is Insufficient

While GAS excels at global semantic alignment, our analysis reveals critical gaps that necessitate additional components in the VCS framework.

Need for Local Alignment Score (LAS)

Problem: No Local Content Assessment

GAS cannot detect specific content omissions, hallucinations, or event-level inaccuracies.

Problem: Lacks Granular Analysis

Global embeddings miss detailed semantic correspondence between specific text segments.

LAS Solution

Chunk-level semantic matching with precision/recall evaluation for detailed content assessment.

Need for Narrative Alignment Score (NAS)

Problem: Chronological Blindness

GAS cannot detect local (0.991) or global (0.981) chronological disruptions that break narrative flow.

Problem: No Temporal Assessment

Global embeddings are insensitive to logical sequence and temporal progression.

NAS Solution

Distance-based and line-based chronological evaluation with flexible tolerance parameters.
